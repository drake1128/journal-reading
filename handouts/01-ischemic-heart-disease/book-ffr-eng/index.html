Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR) Lecture Notes Theoretical Foundation 1.1 FFR Definition and Principles Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR) is a physiological index used to assess the hemodynamic significance of coronary artery stenosis. 1.1.1 Basic Definition FFR = Pd Pa (1) Where: • Pd = Distal coronary pressure (pressure distal to stenosis) • Pa = Aortic pressure (proximal pressure) 1.1.2 Theoretical Derivation FFR represents the ratio of maximum achievable blood flow in the presence of stenosis to the theoretical max- imum flow without stenosis in the myocardial territory supplied by that coronary artery. According to Poiseuille’s law, blood flow is proportional to the pressure gradient: Q = ∆P R (2) Under maximal hyperemia (induced by adenosine): FFR = Qmax,stenosis Qmax,normal = (Pd −Pv)/Rmyo (Pa −Pv)/Rmyo ≈Pd Pa (3) When Pv (central venous pressure) is much smaller than Pa and Pd, this simplifies to FFR = Pd/Pa. 1.1.3 Clinical Significance Table 1: Clinical Interpretation of FFR Values FFR Value Clinical Significance FFR > 0.80 No hemodynamic significance, PCI not recommended FFR ≤0.80 Hemodynamically significant, PCI recommended FFR < 0.75 Definite ischemia, strong recommendation for intervention 0.75 < FFR < 0.80 Gray zone, requires clinical judgment FFR < 0.50 Severe ischemia, high-risk lesion Technical Aspects 2.1 Measurement Technique 2.1.1 Equipment Requirements • Pressure wire • Hyperemic agents: Adenosine (140-180 µg/kg/min IV) or IC bolus (40-80 µg) • Pressure measurement system • Angiography equipment

Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR) Lecture Notes 2.1.2 Measurement Protocol Table 2: Standard FFR Measurement Procedure Step Operational Details Calibration: Zero the pressure wire and measurement system Equalization: Confirm Pd = Pa at guide catheter position Wire advancement: Advance pressure wire distal to lesion Induce hyperemia: Administer adenosine for maximal hyperemia Record pressure: Record Pd and Pa during hyperemia Calculate FFR: Automatic or manual calculation of FFR = Pd/Pa Pullback verification: Withdraw wire to guide catheter to check for drift 2.2 Non-Hyperemic Indices 2.2.1 iFR (Instantaneous Wave-free Ratio) iFR is measured during a specific period of the cardiac cycle (wave-free period) without hyperemic agents: • Measurement timing: Mid-diastole (approximately 25% of cardiac cycle) • Cutoff: iFR ≤0.89 considered significant • Advantages: No adenosine required, shorter procedure time, fewer side effects • Correlation with FFR: Approximately 80-85% concordance Wheatstone Bridge Principle 3.1 Application to Pressure Measurement Pressure wire systems utilize the Wheatstone Bridge principle to convert mechanical pressure into electrical signals. 3.1.1 Basic Bridge Structure The bridge consists of four resistors, with at least one being a pressure-sensitive resistor (piezoresistive sensor): Vout = Vin ( R3 R3 + R4 − R2 R1 + R2 ) (4) When pressure changes, the resistance of the pressure-sensitive element changes, causing a voltage output variation that allows pressure measurement. 3.1.2 Application in FFR Systems Table 3: Wheatstone Bridge Components in FFR Systems Component Function Pressure sensor Located at wire tip, contains piezoresistive elements Signal conversion Converts pressure changes to voltage changes Amplifier Amplifies minute voltage signals Calibration system Eliminates temperature, drift, and other interference Digital conversion Converts analog signals to digital pressure readings

Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR) Lecture Notes 3.2 Technical Considerations • Drift: Prolonged measurements may cause signal drift; calibration before and after is essential • Temperature compensation: Body temperature variations can affect resistance values • Sensitivity: Modern pressure wires can detect pressure changes within ±2 mmHg

Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR) Lecture Notes Conclusions FFR represents a major advancement in percutaneous coronary intervention, providing an objective physiolog- ical assessment standard. Key conclusions include: 1. Stable CAD: FFR-guided PCI superior to pure angiography-guided approach (FAME 1 & 2) 2.
