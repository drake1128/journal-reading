## 前言 Introduction


經皮冠狀動脈介入術(Percutaneous Coronary Intervention, PCI)的成功關鍵之一在於導絲(guidewire)能否順利通過病灶。不同的病灶型態需要選擇適當的導絲,並運用正確的技術才能安全有效地完成手術。

The success of Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI) critically depends on whether the guidewire can successfully cross the lesion. Different lesion morphologies require appropriate guidewire selection and correct techniques to safely and effectively complete the procedure.

本講義將詳細介紹導絲選擇的原則、polymer jacket導絲的特性,以及3D wiring method等進階技術,幫助臨床醫師提升PCI的成功率與安全性。

This manual will provide detailed introduction to guidewire selection principles, characteristics of polymer jacket guidewires, and advanced techniques such as 3D wiring method, helping clinicians improve the success rate and safety of PCI.

#### 臨床考量 Clinical Considerations


importantbox
**關鍵概念 Key Concepts**

在鈣化病灶中,導絲通過彎曲或鈣化區域時會遇到困難。除非鈣化病灶的狹窄非常嚴重,否則操作並不太困難。然而,近端彎曲會對導絲產生極大阻力,使得在鈣化病灶內的操作變得困難。

In calcified lesions, the guidewire encounters difficulty when crossing tortuous or calcified areas. Unless the calcified lesion has very severe stenosis, the operation is not too difficult. However, proximal tortuosity creates significant resistance to the guidewire, making manipulation within the calcified lesion challenging.

****重要警告:****過度施力可能導致進入false lumen的風險!

****Important Warning:**** Excessive force may lead to the risk of entering the false lumen!

#### 安全策略 Safety Strategy


**最佳實踐建議 Best Practice Recommendations**


1. **最小coating原則 Minimal Coating Principle:**        使用最少量hydrophilic coating或polymer jacket的導絲可防止操作壓力和不必要的併發症        Using guidewires with minimal hydrophilic coating or polymer jacket can prevent operational stress and unnecessary complications

#### 導絲選擇原則 Guidewire Selection Principles


**特殊考量 Special Considerations**

雖然hydrophilic coating導絲容易通過病灶,但放置支架時常需要將導引導管從冠狀動脈開口移出。因此,****無hydrophilic coating的導絲較為適合****。

Although hydrophilic-coated guidewires easily cross lesions, guide catheter withdrawal from the coronary ostium is often necessary during stent placement. Therefore, ****non-hydrophilic coated guidewires are more suitable****.

若因遠端血管型態不得不使用強coating導絲,基於安全理由應在適當時機更換導絲。

If a heavily coated wire must be used due to distal vessel anatomy, it should be exchanged at an appropriate time for safety reasons.

## Polymer Jacket導絲  
 Polymer Jacket Guidewires


### 定義與結構 Definition and Structure


PCI使用的導絲依結構分為金屬基底型和polymer jacket型。Polymer是由多個單體聚合而成的化合物,通常為高分子量有機化合物。以polymer sleeve包覆金屬內芯並施加hydrophilic coating的產品稱為polymer jacket導絲。

Guidewires used in PCI are classified into metal-based and polymer jacket types based on their structure. Polymers are compounds formed by polymerization of multiple monomers, typically high molecular weight organic compounds. Products with a polymer sleeve covering the metal core and applied with hydrophilic coating are called polymer jacket guidewires.


### 特性 Characteristics


#### 優異的滑動特性 Excellent Lubricity


importantbox
**核心特點 Core Feature**

Polymer jacket導絲最重要的特點是其****滑順性(lubricity)****。

The most important feature of polymer jacket guidewires is their ****lubricity****.

**優點 Advantages:**

- 能夠滑入金屬導絲無法到達的病灶        Can slide into lesions unreachable by metallic wires


**缺點 Disadvantages:**

- 會減弱觸感,較難傳達血管和病灶的特性        Reduces tactile feedback, making it harder to convey vessel and lesion characteristics


importantbox


#### 導絲尖端負荷 Tip Load


**Tip Load的定義 Definition of Tip Load**

此處的tip load不同於製造商標示的數值,而是指導絲尖端數毫米到1公分範圍內的硬度。

The tip load here differs from manufacturer-specified values and refers to the stiffness within a few millimeters to 1 cm from the wire tip.

**形成原因 Formation Mechanism:**

因polymer施加在柔軟金屬線圈表面,導絲尖端意外地較硬。

Because polymer is applied to the surface of a soft metallic coil, the wire tip is unexpectedly stiff.

**評估方式 Assessment Method:**

最簡單的評估方式:一手握住導絲尖端附近,另一手手指墊感受尖端硬度。

The simplest assessment method: Hold the wire near its tip with one hand and feel the tip stiffness with the finger pad of the other hand.


### 使用時機 Indications for Use


| p{5cm}p{8cm}}   **序號** | **臨床情境    Clinical Scenario** | **理由    Rationale** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 鈣化狹窄病灶    Calcified stenotic lesions | 因滑順性使導絲更容易通過,金屬導絲因摩擦力難以通過    Lubricity facilitates crossing; metallic wires struggle due to friction |
| 2 | 支架網眼上的側枝選擇    Side branch selection through stent struts | 支架網眼對導絲產生摩擦阻力,polymer jacket導絲更易插入側枝    Stent struts create frictional resistance; polymer jacket wires enter side branches more easily |
| 3 | 彎曲血管    Tortuous vessels | 連續之字形彎曲和高度曲率使得血管壁對導絲摩擦阻力高    Continuous zigzag tortuosity and high curvature create high frictional resistance |
| 4 | Reverse wire method | 用於將導絲插入從主幹以鈍角分支的側枝,或狹窄病灶後立即分支的側枝(尤其是偏心病灶)    Used for inserting wire into side branches at obtuse angles or immediately after stenotic lesions |
| 5 | STAR技術    STAR technique | Subintimal tracking and re-entry (STAR)技術涉及有意的subintimal tracking    Involves intentional subintimal tracking and re-entry |


### 使用注意事項 Precautions for Use


importantbox
****重要警告 Important Warnings****

**1. 滲入斑塊 Plaque Penetration**

因滑順性和局部較硬的尖端,容易滑入脆弱斑塊。若未注意而推進至周邊,可能造成dissection。

Due to lubricity and locally stiff tip, it easily slides into vulnerable plaques. If advanced peripherally without attention, it may cause dissection.

**特別注意:**在急性冠狀動脈症候群等不穩定、脆弱斑塊病灶需特別小心。

**Special attention:** Exercise particular caution in unstable, vulnerable plaque lesions such as acute coronary syndrome.
importantbox

importantbox
****重要警告 Important Warnings (續)****

**2. 氣球擴張後的病灶通過 Crossing Post-Balloon Dilated Lesions**

病灶經氣球擴張後會產生人工dissection。導絲尖端進入dissection腔會導致dissection擴大。

Lesions create iatrogenic dissection after balloon dilation. Wire tip entry into the dissection cavity will cause dissection extension.

**3. 穿孔風險 Perforation Risk**


- 可無阻力穿透小的周邊血管        Can penetrate small peripheral vessels without resistance
- 因尖端較硬,容易發生穿孔        Perforations occur easily due to stiff tip
- 必須始終注意確保尖端在正確位置        Must always ensure tip is in correct position
- 與microcatheter併用進行CTO穿透時,導絲尖端也會受到強大作用力,需謹慎操作        When used with microcatheter for CTO crossing, wire tip experiences strong forces requiring careful manipulation

## 3D Wiring Method


### 正交方向影像 Orthogonal Directions


3D wiring method的核心概念是使用兩個約90度角的影像角度來精確導引導絲。

The core concept of 3D wiring method is using two approximately 90-degree angulated image projections to precisely guide the guidewire.

### 臨床應用關鍵 Key Clinical Applications


#### 2D vs 3D Wiring的適用時機  
 When to Use 2D vs 3D Wiring


| p{2.5cm}p{8.5cm}}   **位置    Location** | **優先方法    Priority** | **說明    Description** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CTO入口    CTO entry | 2D > 3D | 導絲位於血管腔內,移動不穩定且難以操控。需旋轉tip curve尋找可插入點    Wire in vessel lumen, unstable and difficult to control. Need to rotate tip curve to find entry point |
| CTO內部    Within CTO | 2D = 3D | 若有landmarks且可維持操作性,應嘗試3D wiring。若無進展,可用3D影像確定約180°方向並擺動尖端    If landmarks present and maneuverability maintained, attempt 3D wiring. If no progress, use 3D imaging to confirm  180° direction |
| CTO出口    CTO exit | 3D > 2D | 可視需要進行選擇性造影,盡可能使用3D wiring達成pinpoint puncture    Perform selective contrast injection as needed, use 3D wiring for pinpoint puncture whenever possible |


#### 3D Wiring穿透技術要點  
 Key Points for 3D Wiring Penetration


importantbox
**導絲推進的必要條件 Prerequisites for Wire Advancement**

**1. 充足的Backup Support**

- 6 Fr或更大的guide catheter        6 Fr or larger guide catheter
- Corsair catheter
- 必要時使用anchor        Use anchor when necessary


**2. 充足的Tip Load**

- 常需要Conquest wire        Often requires Conquest wire
- 對出口處的纖維囊尤其重要        Especially important for fibrous cap at exit
- 仔細考慮尖端推進的縱向向量並進行第一次穿刺        Carefully consider longitudinal vector of tip advancement for first puncture


importantbox


#### 穿透過程中的處理 Management During Penetration


**情境一:導管無法通過硬組織  
 Scenario 1: Catheter Cannot Cross Hard Tissue**


1. 嘗試增加tip load        Attempt to increase tip load
2. 在3D影像下稍微改變穿刺點        Slightly modify puncture point under 3D imaging
3. 若仍無效,考慮替代路徑或視情況在出口處刻意旋轉尖端        If still unsuccessful, consider alternative route or deliberately rotate tip at exit


**情境二:尖端在出口處容易推進但偏離預定位置  
 Scenario 2: Tip Advances Easily at Exit but Deviates**

****懷疑導絲移入false lumen****


- 從false lumen穿刺至true lumen幾乎不可能        Puncture from false to true lumen nearly impossible
- 可從前方重新推進導絲        May re-advance wire from antegrade approach
- 或使用IVUS guided或retrograde approach        Or use IVUS-guided or retrograde approach


### 臨床實踐秘訣 Clinical Practice Tips


importantbox
****實踐智慧 Practice Wisdom****

對於CTO病灶,應嘗試使用Gaia和Conquest wires的3D wiring method。然而,實際上會處理各種病灶,3D wiring method不應被視為唯一推薦技術且不應固守。

For CTO lesions, attempt 3D wiring method with Gaia and Conquest wires. However, in practice various lesions are encountered, and 3D wiring method should not be considered the only recommended technique nor rigidly adhered to.

****關鍵原則:****重要的是根據情況適當地結合使用2D和3D wiring method。

****Key Principle:**** What is important is appropriately combining 2D and 3D wiring methods based on the situation.

## 總結 Summary


### 核心要點回顧 Core Points Review


1. **導絲選擇應基於病灶特性    Guidewire Selection Based on Lesion Characteristics**      - 近端彎曲:選擇操控性佳的導絲            Proximal tortuosity: choose wires with good handling - 鈣化病灶:需要滑順性和支撐力的平衡            Calcified lesions: balance lubricity and support - 開口病灶:優先使用無coating導絲            Ostial lesions: prioritize non-coated wires
2. **Polymer Jacket導絲的雙面性    Dual Nature of Polymer Jacket Wires**      - 優點:優異的滑順性,能通過困難病灶            Advantages: excellent lubricity, crosses difficult lesions - 缺點:穿孔和dissection風險增加            Disadvantages: increased perforation and dissection risk - 使用後應適時更換為安全性較高的導絲            Should be exchanged for safer wires when appropriate
3. **3D Wiring Method的靈活應用    Flexible Application of 3D Wiring**      - 在CTO出口處特別有價值            Particularly valuable at CTO exit - 需與2D method靈活搭配            Requires flexible combination with 2D method - 充足的backup和適當的tip load是成功關鍵            Adequate backup and appropriate tip load are keys to success
4. **安全性始終是首要考量    Safety is Always the Primary Consideration**      - 避免過度施力            Avoid excessive force - 密切監測導絲位置            Closely monitor wire position - 必要時毫不猶豫地更換導絲            Exchange wire without hesitation when necessary
