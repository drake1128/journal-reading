frame{Procedural Strategy - ISR Management}
block{Treatment Options for ISR Total Occlusion}

1. **Balloon Angioplasty:**      - Drug-coated balloon (DCB) preferred - High-pressure balloon inflation
2. **Repeat Stenting:**      - DES with different drug/polymer - Consider bioresorbable scaffold
3. **Atherectomy:**      - Rotational atherectomy if calcified - Cutting balloon for focal ISR
4. **Brachytherapy:** For recurrent ISR (if available)


block
frame

frame{Recanalization Strategy}
block{Approach for Total Occlusion in Stent}

1. **Antegrade Approach:**      - Start with soft polymer-jacketed wires - Progress to stiffer wires if needed - Use microcatheter support
2. **Retrograde Approach:**      - Via RV branch collateral - Consider if antegrade fails
3. **Imaging Guidance:**      - IVUS to assess stent expansion/apposition - OCT for tissue characterization


block

frame{Potential Complications}
alertblock{ISR-Specific Risks}

- **Perforation:** Risk with aggressive wire manipulation
- **Distal embolization:** Tissue prolapse through stent struts
- **Stent deformation:** From high-pressure balloon inflation
- **No-reflow:** Microembolization of neointimal tissue
- **Acute recoil:** After balloon deflation
- **Early re-restenosis:** Higher risk in diabetic patients


alertblock>
